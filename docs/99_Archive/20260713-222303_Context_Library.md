> Digital Twin component. First real content 2026-07-07, from 43 backfilled sessions (May 7 – Jul 7, mode=HIST, 14,225 snapshots). Base rates are the library's foundation: they tell every future daily review what is NORMAL, so narratives stop treating routine events as remarkable.

## Session base rates (n=43, MNQ, 24h sessions)
- **Day range:** median 576 pts (IQR 402–720; max 1,588). A "big day" for MNQ in this era starts above ~720.
- **Value-area width:** ~171–181 pts typical.
- **Value relocation (POC jump >30 pts): occurs on 100% of days; median 5 events/day; 86% of days have ≥3.** Relocation is routine, not exceptional — daily reviews must stop narrating single relocations as defining events unless they exceed base rates.
- **Yet median total POC travel is only 88 pts/day.** Reconciliation: the POC frequently OSCILLATES between competing high-volume nodes (double-distribution flip-flop) rather than migrating monotonically. Consequence: 'value migration' needs a persistence criterion, and `valueMigAgeMin` resets on flips (see M-11).
